

# IDLE COLONY REVIEWS:

Hasn't been tooo successful, only 400 reviews on steam.

Here's some reviews:




## SUPER IMPORTANT REVIEW:
It is an alright game, very fun however it is very short and lacking depth and content. The idea and execution are good it just feels like it needs to be fleshed out a bit more, I let it idle for one day after playing actively for maybe 3-5 hours, and I came back to having beaten the game. I just wish there was more to do and explore, more mechanics and maps and colonists and stuff to find. I appreciate the challenges however the draw of the game isn't to beat the game in a handicapped manner but to find the best and optimal way to progress through an interesting world and concept. Good game overall however not necessarily worth the money for the amount of content.








## POSITIVE-STEAM-REVIEW: (18 hours)
I'm quite neutral. I was very hesitant on buying this game. It's not a BAD game, but these idlers that pull you in just to somewhat disappoint you in the end are getting quite annoying. Idle Colony pulls you in like Gnorp with its mechanics of stuff flying everywhere that makes your brain feel good, like a number going up, and that all stops when you beat the game in under 8 hours and the rest of your time will be achievement farming. I was hoping for more time spent in the game before reaching the end.

Pros
Satisfying to watch.
Doing a Prestige (Lunar) reset doesn't take long to reach where you were at.
Achievement farming game.
After beating the game there's still a bit more content left.

Cons
Game is pretty buggy as you progress. Memory leak when you have a lot of stuff going on and I have 75 farmers, 40+ of which aren't doing anything. You're forced to restart the game every hour or so.
Only able to set 3 upgrades to auto-buy instead of all upgrades.
Always micro-managing since auto-buy is limited.
Can beat the game in/under 8 hours.
UI can be very annoying.
Settings for "1x 5x Max." doesn't save.

Idle Colony is strictly a flashy achievement farm game. It's one of those typical idlers that look VERY good and it ends up not lasting long at all. I'll give it a thumbs up seeing I'll be farming the rest of the achievements and spend more time in the game, but I really wish Steam had an in-between option.



## POSITIVE STEAM REVIEW - 2:
I would give this a neutral vote if I could. It's neat in that there is a ton of stuff going on all the time which makes upgrading everything a fun visual treat as you progress. That said I find some of the mechanics to be a little underwhelming or difficult to enjoy. My least favorite mechanic is the manually set paths; I want these little numbnuts to go towards towers and highest tier trees automatically which doesn't seem that challenging to do. I also want my incremental games to have more prestige options so each new run is more powerful; this game has some skills load outs you can distribute points into but nothing to give flat % gains to various stuff each run, at least I think they don't I couldn't figure that out. Also I had some menus failing to respond late-game due to all the stuff happening on the screen.

Basically for the price it's OK.

## NEGATIVE REVIEW (10 hours)
I wish each meta-progression point felt more impactful. There have been multiple times I've prestiged, but since the upgrades I could get only affect super specific things, the next run feels exactly the same.


## NEGATIVE-STEAM-REVIEW 1:
I'd gladly recommend the first few hours as a fidget toy while watching TV, but the game falls off very quickly after you "beat" it, even though that's where the unique tech upgrades can be accessed.

I first played Idle Colony before release, back when it was just a demo, and I want to like it so so so much. I like the visual style and the quirky mechanics in the game/tech tree, but progression is just too slow for me to recommend it as anything other than a 4-6 hour fidget toy.

The biggest downside is that the tech tree/passive upgrades do very little to accelerate subsequent runs. Post credits, when every upgrade was accessible, it still takes me 10 minutes of fully paying attention to the game to get the first solar coin each run, which doesn't feel good.

If Glory or meta progression points did something to boot income, if I could upgrade to more than 3 auto-buy slots, if I could get the impact of "Super Cursor" without my mouse having to be hovering over the screen, if I could more reliably get tier 18-20 saplings when striving for tier 50-55 fruit, I'd be way more likely to stick with the game and try out more of the tech/upgrades.

Right now, it feels like each reset means I have to spend 30 minutes grinding through the same early stages before I can experience anything different from previous runs, and that just isn't fun.




## NEGATIVE STEAM REVIEW (13 hours)
Giving this a negative review primarily because the name of the game is very misleading. Normally, that could be ignored if the game was incredible, but it's pretty "meh" even for what it is.

This game is not idle, not even close. You can go idle, sure, but you won't progress fast at all. I will say though that the game progresses fast enough that you don't need much "idle" time.

However, that's not why this review is negative. The game itself is just very "blegh". There's supposed to be strategies involved with every prestige point you unlock, but realistically speaking only a handful are viable, and some require an annoying amount of micromanagement. All I found myself doing eventually was giving myself carpal tunnel trying to buy every unlock as soon as it became available, because there's not much reason you don't want to buy any upgrades (only certain strategies like giant colonists, gotta not buy houses), then....number goes up until you feel like it's impossible to progress without prestiging, but then that leads to the next major problem:

The prestige perks are so bland. By prestige level 7 I was running out of anything that looked appealing. Most, if not all prestige points have pros and cons, but there aren't even any difficult choices to make. After a while, I had prestige points sitting there because there were no choices that even looked appealing. It doesn't help that the overall goal of the game, the "fruit gathered", is so difficult to figure out what's working and what isn't. Do you want a few higher tier fruits or spam lower tiers? Does it even matter if I decide to utilize the "Idle" aspect and come back to the next upgrade tier? Since this is a type of game that there's no way to "fail", it's just you waiting to see if you're progressing fast enough before you run out of patience for the existing run.

Overall, by the time you get fed up with the game, it'll be hard to argue you didn't get your money's worth (this is probably the best way I justify anyone getting this). If you're looking for "Gnorp 2, Overwhelmingly Positive", you're going to be disappointed. If you don't mind a "We have Gnorp at home" experience, then this might be worth your time.


## NEGATIVE REVIEW (8 hours)
Boring, too slow. There's no hook compared to the great idle games. You'll be waiting minutes to make a dent in progression. Free idle games were more entertaining. I'd refund if I could but I figured I just didn't see enough to get to the fun.

At 7.5 hours, I'm giving up on finding the fun.
