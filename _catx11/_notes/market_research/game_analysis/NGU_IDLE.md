# NEGATIVE-REVIEW-1:
NGU Idle suffers from the same thing most of the games of this genre suffer from: your progress keeps slowing down and down and down and down.
Not only that, but basic QoL features are locked behind hundreds of hours of grind, or real money payments.
It doesn't help that the game already starts slow compared to most idle games. 
With games like Cookie Clicker, you can reasonably get a week maybe two in before you're bored, 
and you barely even have to interact with the game, so you just kinda watch the silly graphics and the number going up, 
like a dopamine screensaver. 

This game, however, other than being actually horrible to look at (besides the boss art) is not even an idle game. 
The "Idle" in "NGU Idle" is false advertising, as I've spent the better part of a week having to babysit the game every 30 minutes. 

This is an incremental game, an incremental game whose increments are microscopic only a few dozen hours in 
and it dangles phantasmal dopamine in front of you like a carrot on a stick. 

It took some time for me to finally realize, but yeah, the carrot is a mirage, and there's only a stick, which you're getting beat up with.
Want a meaningful incremental game experience? Buy "Nodebuster" or "Digseum", they're short, but very fun and well designed!


# NEGATIVE-REVIEW-2:
This review will contain spoilers for in-game mechanics, later down in it. Consider this the spoiler warning.

Overall, I can only half-heartedly recommend this game. Allow me to explain. The game takes place over three difficulties: Normal, Evil, and Sadistic. As you play through the game, you can tell where 4G dropped the ball, late Evil. Titan 9 for quite a while had no feature, as Sadistic was being developed into. When we got that feature, and most of Sadistic flushed out, 4G abandoned this game for his other, leaving the game largely unfinished. Now that we have what reads as the final update, this review can take place over the game as a whole (using my 571 days of playtime as judge).

Normal is fun. Even Evil is fun. If you intend to play this game, stop there (or a little into Sadistic). The quality of work begins to drop off rapidly as you move into Sadistic. Some Evil challenges, and most Sadistic challenges, still have no rewards. There is no more story for bosses. And I would be remiss without mentioning the final feature of the game.

Cooking requires that you fiddle with ingredients to try and reach (reportedly) 100% efficiency, and eat your meal to get an EXP gain multiplier. The problem is that with your only indication being "when I add this it go up/down", your only method is click and pray. Even cycling through ingredients finding local maxima, I have yet to hit the 100% that you can supposedly get on every dish. This feature is without a doubt, a let down to the community who waited more than half a year for this update. The only feature I can compare this to is MacGuffins, a late Normal feature that more or less sucks until you reach Sadistic and get its scaling improvement (as otherwise you're limited to 30 min rebirths, which doesn't line-up with the 24 hr rebirth cycle that a couple of other major features run on).

While the way this game has been treated in terms of development has been horrible in the recent-ish past, there are some good qualities. Normal, and most of Evil, are well worth playing. It just so happens that these are also the finished parts of the game (Evil challenges notwithstanding). I recommend playing these, and nothing more.

In conclusion: game starts out great, goes on pretty strong, then becomes a disappointment.

# POSITIVE-REVIEW-1:
The genre that (mostly) began with Cookie Clicker reaches its logical conclusion.

What makes Borderlands and World of Warcraft and all of those other looty grindy games appealing? The dopamine hit that your brain releases when you see those numbers on your stats go up! Why bother with AAA developers, lootboxes, season passes, fancy graphics and contrived plotlines when you can cut out the middleman and simply watch NUMBERS GO UP?

NGU IDLE is an amazing timesink. The game revolves around a small-ish number of stats and resources, but quickly increases the way all of these numbers interact with each other. While the main goal of increasing numbers remains the same, the player will quickly have to learn the best way to manage. At first glance the new player may be overwhelmed, but those who have a predilection for numbers and how they interact will have a ton of fun figuring it all out.

As the game goes on, you'll uncover the main storyline (if you're into that kind of thing) and progress from zone to zone finding better and better gear. You'll unlock the ability to enhance your stats through advanced training and augmentation before you take on your first TITAN, one of the true bosses of the game. Defeating these titans unlocks even more ways your numbers can interact with each other. These are best left discovered by the player, but rest assured you won't be disappointed as you watch your numbers climb into the stratosphere.

With a very active developer and community, you'll find help is easy to come by when you have a question about how a mechanic works or what to emphasize next.

NGU IDLE is truly the best thing to leave running on your computer and actually end up paying at least $10/mo for on your electric bill. 10/10 would waste earth's precious natural resources again


# POSITIVE-REVIEW-2:
I just finished The End, so the playtime you see is how long it took me. The run wasn't "optimal", but sped up in that a guide was used and some money spent.

It's the best "idle" "game" I've ever "played", although all of those words are wrong.

It's like owning a garden. You don't "play" a garden. You have daily tasks like weeding, watering, tending and harvesting and while there's some enjoyment to be had in these things, the real payoff is when you leave and later return to find interesting things have happened and new things need doing.

NGU has been part of my life for well over a year. My morning ritual was waking up early and tend to it before leaving for work. At night I came home to review the results. Rinse and repeat.

Now I feel like I finished a good book. A little sad and feeling like I just said goodbye to an old friend.

I will miss it now that it's over.

In the end, this silly bit of nonsense took my time and in exchange gave me enjoyment from watching it's numbers go up, and the comfort that comes from following a steady, faithful daily routine.

I hope it does the same for you.
